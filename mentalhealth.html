In this project investigating the interplay between social media and mental health, the connection between online user behavior and depression was explored. Utilizing a diverse set of machine learning and deep learning algorithms, including CNN, RNN, LSTM, Na√Øve Bayes, and Gradient Boosting, the study aimed to assess the significance of sentiment polarity in Twitter data. The application of NLTK for pre-processing laid the foundation for these analyses. Notably, the Convolutional Neural Network (CNN) emerged as the most effective model, boasting an impressive accuracy of 0.89. This result underscores CNN's proficiency in capturing and interpreting sentiment patterns within the intricate landscape of social media, offering crucial insights into the nuanced relationship between online content and mental health.
